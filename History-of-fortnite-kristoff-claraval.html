History Of Fortnite

A Look into the Origins, Evolution, and Future of Fortnite

Fortnite is one of the most well-known brands,
not only in the videogame space but in pop
culture as a whole. Developed by Epic Games ,
this cultural phenomenon has significantly
impacted the gaming industry since its release
in 2017. Home to some of the most high-profile
collaborations in entertainment, Fortnite helped
reshape the way people perceive and engage with
video games, making it one of the most successful
live service games in the process. But Fortnite
was not always the household name it is today.
So, what triggered its meteoric rise and how has
the company managed to keep the game relevant in
an industry that evolves every day? Let’s take
a look at the fascinating story of Fortnite’s
origin, evolution, and promising future.



Fortnite started as a cooperative, PvE game titled
"Fortnite: Save the World." Released in July 2017,
Save the World allowed players to team up with other
players, gather resources, and build forts during the
day while fending off waves of zombie-like creatures
during the night in a tower-defense style gameplay.
This blend of genres, though promising, did not garner
widespread attention. For a multiplayer title, player
engagement makes or breaks the game, and it was the
lukewarm reception of the original game that prompted
Epic Games to repurpose the game’s map and introduce
the Battle Royale mode.

A game mode that was rapidly gaining popularity thanks
to the success of the then-recently released Player
Unknown’s Battleground or PUBG. Although PUBG had been
around for about six months at this point, the game had
its fair share of issues despite being a massive success.
While Fortnite borrowed elements from PUBG, it also
featured the building mechanic from the original game
which set it apart. Fortnite Battle Royale took the gaming
world by storm. Its vibrant graphics and accessibility
attracted a massive and diverse player base.




But Fortnite's success was not solely based on its gameplay.
The game transcended traditional gaming boundaries, becoming
a cultural phenomenon with a substantial impact on mainstream
media. Epic Games implemented a dynamic strategy of constant
updates and collaborations with popular franchises, such as
Marvel Studios , DC Comics , and musicians like Travis Scott.
These crossovers elevated Fortnite beyond a mere video game,
transforming it into a platform for cultural events. Also,
the game's live in-game events, such as the rocket launch and
the black hole event, captured global attention. Concurrently,
Fortnite entered the realm of eSports with significant prize
pools, attracting professional players and audiences alike.

To maintain its momentum, Epic Games introduced the concept of
"chapters," dividing the game's seasons into thematic segments
with evolving storylines. This approach kept players engaged
and provided a fresh gaming experience with regular updates.
Additionally, Fortnite expanded its reach by being accessible
on multiple platforms, including PC, consoles, and mobile devices.

Perhaps the biggest leap forward for Fortnite is the introduction
of Unreal Editor for Fortnite or UEFN. In March 2023, Epic Games
released UEFN to the masses, empowering prospective game developers,
and many others to create their games and release them within
Fortnite with the ability to earn revenue using Epic Games’s revenue
sharing program. This made Fortnite transcend from a game to a
platform that hosts various interesting experiences.

Today, a significant number of studios, alongside independent
developers and students create content for Fortnite with many of
them seeing great success. Epic Games itself has enhanced its
portfolio with the addition of games of various other genres like
Rocket Racing, Fortnite Festival, and Lego Fortnite, all of which
seamlessly let the player earn XP and spend them in-game, once
again keeping them engaged in the process.


Today, Fortnite stands as a force to be reckoned with. It is, by
definition, the biggest crossover event in the history of
entertainment. One that can have Kratos do the floss dance, while
Captain America, John Wick, and Peter Griffin team up with him in
a Squads match. One that forced a mega-corporation like Sony to
bend their rules for cross-play. Looking ahead, one can only expect
the brand to continuously evolve as it has since 2017, and the
possibilities seem endless.

Perhaps Fortnite becomes the first to shape the metaverse in a
meaningful way. Or it could find a way to integrate full-blown AAA
experiences within the platform. Epics Game’s next move to enhance
Fortnite may be a mystery at this point but given the company’s hot
streak with the brand, we know that whatever is coming is sure worth
being excited for.

Fortnite's evolution from a cooperative survival game to a cultural
and gaming phenomenon showcases the dynamic nature of the video game
industry. Its ability to adapt, innovate, and capture the zeitgeist
has set new standards for online gaming experiences. Whether it be
through in-game events, collaborations, its impact on the eSports
scene, or its transformation into a platform for thousands of developers
big and small, Fortnite has left an indelible mark on the gaming
landscape, solidifying its place as one of the most influential games
of the 21st century.
